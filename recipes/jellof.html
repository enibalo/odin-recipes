Jollof

Description

Jollof is a delicious nigerian dish commonly eaten at parties and at weddings. Jollof is a little challenging to make without burning some rice so don't feel bad if you do. I hope one person looking at this website gets so good at making jellof that they can make it based on muscle-memory. Or should we say ktichen-memory... Even if you aren't great at cooking I feel that this is a recipe that you can give a shot! I love jellof and I hope you will love it too after trying to make it yourself.

Jollof is essentially rice that absorbs a blended pepper mix, instead of spiced water like most recipes you may have tried before. So, you may notice that getting the temperature and cooking-time tight might take you a few tries. As I said previously, you will probaly not escape without a burnt layer at the bottom on your first try so be WARNED!

Ingredients

  • Tomatoes
  • Red bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Maggi Cubes or All-Purpose Seasoning
  • Salt
  • Thyme
  • Curry powder
  • Bay leaves
  • Water
  • Rice
  • Oil
  • Tomato paste
  • Chicken broth

Steps

  1. Blend onion, peppers, and tomatoes.
  2. Parboil rice for 10-15 minutes with plain water.
  3. Put oil in a pot and fry a chopped up onion inside for a few minutes. Then add the tomato paste to the mixture and fry for a few more minutes. Gas on medium heat.
  4. Add in the blended pepper mixture and leave it on gas until oil and pepper have mixed together properly. (No longer separated).
  5. Add in chicken broth leave on gas for a few minutes.
  6. Taste chicken broth, then add seasonings as you see fit.
  7. Finally add rice. Cook until rice is soft.
